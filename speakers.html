Keynote Speakers

Leading experts in eye-tracking and NLP research

Featured Speakers

Meet the brilliant minds that will be sharing their insights at Gaze4NLP 2025

Keynote

Prof. Emily Watson

Professor of Cognitive Science

Stanford University, USA

Prof. Watson is a pioneer in the field of eye-tracking research for language processing. Her work has significantly contributed to our understanding of how humans read and comprehend text, with over 150 publications in top-tier journals. She currently leads the Cognitive Language Processing Lab at Stanford University.

Website | Twitter | LinkedIn

Eye Movements as a Window into Language Processing: Past, Present, and Future

This talk will explore the rich history of using eye movements to study language comprehension and how current advances in NLP can benefit from eye-tracking methodologies, with special attention to recent advances in multimodal learning.

Day 1 | May 10, 2025 | 09:30 - 10:30

Keynote

Dr. James Chen

Principal Research Scientist

AI Research Institute, Canada

Dr. Chen's research focuses on integrating cognitive signals, particularly eye-tracking data, into neural language models. His groundbreaking work on attention mechanisms inspired by human reading patterns has led to significant improvements in language understanding tasks. Prior to joining the AI Research Institute, he was a senior researcher at Google Brain.

Website | Twitter | Google Scholar

Bridging Human and Machine Attention: Lessons from Eye-Tracking for Large Language Models

This keynote will discuss how insights from human visual attention during reading can inform the development of more interpretable and human-like attention mechanisms in large language models, with practical demonstrations and future research directions.

Day 2 | May 11, 2025 | 09:30 - 10:30

Keynote

Prof. Maria Rodriguez

Director, Human-Computer Interaction Lab

Technical University of Berlin, Germany

Prof. Rodriguez is an internationally recognized expert in multimodal interaction, with a focus on eye-tracking applications in adaptive interfaces. Her research combines eye movement analysis with natural language processing to create responsive systems that adapt to users' cognitive states and needs. She has received numerous awards for her contributions to accessible technology.

Website | Twitter | ResearchGate

Gaze-Aware Interfaces: Personalizing Language Technologies through Eye-Tracking

This talk will explore how eye-tracking can enable more personalized and adaptive language technologies, from reading assistants to communication aids for people with disabilities, highlighting recent advances and ethical considerations in this rapidly evolving field.

Day 3 | May 12, 2025 | 09:30 - 10:30

Invited Speaker

Dr. Thomas Lee

Lead Research Engineer

Language Technologies Inc., USA

With a background in both computational linguistics and cognitive psychology, Dr. Lee has been instrumental in developing practical applications of eye-tracking data for the evaluation and improvement of language models. His team has created innovative tools for analyzing reader engagement and comprehension that are widely used in educational technology.

Website | LinkedIn

From Lab to Market: Commercial Applications of Gaze-Enhanced NLP

This presentation will showcase real-world applications of eye-tracking in natural language processing products, including reading assistants, content optimization tools, and educational technologies, with insights into the challenges and opportunities of bringing this research to market.

Day 2 | May 11, 2025 | 14:00 - 15:00

Invited Speaker

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Associate Professor of Psychology

University of Technology, Australia

Dr. Johnson's research focuses on cross-linguistic differences in reading patterns and their implications for language processing. Her pioneering work with multilingual readers has provided valuable insights into how different writing systems affect eye movements during reading, with important applications for machine translation and literacy development.

Website | Twitter | Google Scholar

Cross-Linguistic Perspectives on Reading: Implications for Multilingual NLP

Drawing on data from readers of diverse writing systems, this talk will explore how eye movement patterns differ across languages and what these differences can teach us about developing more effective multilingual and cross-lingual NLP models.

Day 1 | May 10, 2025 | 14:00 - 15:00

Panel Chair

Prof. David Kim

Director, Center for Language & Cognition

National University, Singapore

Prof. Kim's interdisciplinary research spans computational linguistics, cognitive science, and human-computer interaction. He has made significant contributions to our understanding of how eye-tracking data can be used to evaluate and improve language models. As the chair of this year's panel discussion, he will facilitate a dialogue on the future of gaze-enhanced NLP.

Website | LinkedIn | ResearchGate

Panel Discussion: The Future of Eye-Tracking in NLP - Challenges and Opportunities

This panel will bring together experts from academia and industry to discuss the current challenges and future directions for integrating eye-tracking data into NLP research and applications, with a focus on ethical considerations, technical limitations, and emerging opportunities.

Day 3 | May 12, 2025 | 14:00 - 15:30
